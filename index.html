While the spirits industry as a whole has been experiencing turbulence in recent years, ready-to-drink expressions are a bright spot. What goes up must come down, or so the saying goes. While much of the spirits industry is on the latter part of this trajectory, one category appears to be solidly bucking the trend: RTDs. Take the US. In 2025, total spirits sales by volume fell by 4%, but spirits-based ready-to-drink (RTD) products grew by 14%, IWSR data confirms. In the US, total beverage alcohol volumes fell by 5% in 2025. RTDs were also down by 1%, IWSR notes, but this was largely due to a 5% drop in malt-based expressions. Across 22 markets, RTDs were the only major alcohol segment in growth , up by 2% in volume. The data says it all – it’s a great time to be an RTD. When it comes to brands, though, which ones are worth seeking out? The Pre-Mixed, RTD & Hard Seltzer Masters looked to celebrate those products most deserving of engagement. Two panels of judges gathered at The Singer Tavern in London to taste this year’s entrants. Melita Kiely, editor-in-chief of The Spirits Business , and chair of The Global Spirits Masters Competitions , led the group. El Mundial 2026 en Estados Unidos, Canadá y México presenta una oportunidad estratégica para las bebidas sin alcohol, ya que los horarios de transmisión tardíos en Europa (21:00 horas o más tarde) desalientan el consumo pesado de alcohol entre aficionados que deben trabajar al día siguiente. The time difference means that, in Europe, many matches will kick off later in the evening - around 9pm or 10pm or even later. For years now, the core attribute of Walmart has seemed to be consistency in a world that has been incredibly volatile. Yet each quarter Walmart has, almost without fail, grown its revenue, improved its profits, taken share in both grocery and general merchandise – and increased its stock price. Incredibly, CFO John David Rainey said on the call that Walmart had absorbed $175 million in fuel costs just for the company’s fulfillment operations. In the broader context of the business, this is not a huge problem. Nothing in the quarter suggests that Walmart’s long-term trajectory has changed all that much. But much of the recent rally in Walmart stock likely was driven by investors looking forward to a time when the external environment wasn’t so volatile, and profits could be driven by the company’s long-term strategy, share gains, and advertising and subscription revenue. What the recent earnings show is that such a time hasn’t yet arrived – and it looks like a few Walmart shareholders simply ran out of patience.
